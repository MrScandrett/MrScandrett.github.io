Alan Turing and The Turing Test

How one mathematician reframed the question: "Can machines think?"

Who Was Alan Turing?

  • RoleBritish mathematician and early computer scientist.
  • 1936Proposed the "Turing machine," a model of universal computation.
  • WWIIHelped break German Enigma codes at Bletchley Park.
  • LegacyLaid the mathematical and conceptual groundwork for modern AI and software.

Why Turing Matters for AI

  • ComputationShowed that one general machine could run any algorithm.
  • Machine intelligenceAsked a practical question instead of arguing definitions.
  • 1950 paper"Computing Machinery and Intelligence" introduced the imitation game.
  • Big ideaIf behavior is indistinguishable from human conversation, intelligence may be present.

The Turing Test (Imitation Game)

A judge chats with two hidden participants: one human and one machine.

How It Works

  • Step 1The judge can only text-chat with each participant.
  • Step 2The human and machine both try to sound convincingly human.
  • Step 3The judge decides which one is the machine.
  • ResultIf judges cannot reliably tell, the machine "passes" the test.

Strengths and Criticisms

  • StrengthBehavior-based and easy to run as an experiment.
  • CriticismGood conversation does not prove understanding or consciousness.
  • Modern viewUseful as one benchmark, but not the only measure of intelligence.
  • Class takeawayDefine what kind of intelligence you are testing before evaluating an AI.

Classroom Activity: Run a Mini Turing Test

Compare human and AI responses using the same prompt set.

  1. Round 1
    Write three prompts

    Use one factual question, one creative prompt, and one ethical scenario.

  2. Round 2
    Collect anonymous answers

    Mix one human response and one AI response for each prompt.

  3. Round 3
    Judge each answer

    Vote on which answer seems human and explain your reasoning.

  4. Round 4
    Reflect

    Decide whether your rubric measured intelligence, style mimicry, or both.

Lesson Outputs

Reflection Write a short claim-evidence paragraph Prompt: Is the Turing Test still useful for evaluating modern AI systems? Use at least two pieces of evidence from class discussion.
