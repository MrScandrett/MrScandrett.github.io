𝄞 Chromatic Scale From C to C

One octave from C up to the next C contains 12 semitone steps and 13 note positions if you count both C's. Starting on P1 and ending on P8, the interval names are: P1, m2, M2, m3, M3, P4, TT, P5, m6, M6, m7, M7, P8.

C Do P1 0 semitones
C# / Db Di m2 1 semitone
D Re M2 2 semitones
D# / Eb Ri m3 3 semitones
E Mi M3 4 semitones
F Fa P4 5 semitones
F# / Gb Fi TT 6 semitones
G Sol P5 7 semitones
G# / Ab Si m6 8 semitones
A La M6 9 semitones
A# / Bb Li m7 10 semitones
B Ti M7 11 semitones
C Do P8 12 semitones
Perfect P1, P4, P5, and P8 are the stable anchor intervals.
Minor m2, m3, m6, and m7 sit one semitone lower than their major partners.
Major M2, M3, M6, and M7 are the brighter forms of those same interval families.
Tritone The 6-semitone midpoint is often labeled TT, A4, or d5.
